01

about.me

I started getting into cybersecurity because I wanted to understand how things break — not just how to build them. Growing up with a curiosity about how networks actually work under the hood, I found myself taking apart configurations just to see what would happen. That curiosity turned into a degree.

I'm currently reading for a BSc (Hons) in Ethical Hacking & Network Security awarded by Coventry University (UK) in partnership with NIBM Sri Lanka, building on a Higher National Diploma and Diploma in Network Engineering. My work sits at the intersection of building and breaking: I design enterprise infrastructure — VLAN segmentation, high-availability topologies, site-to-site VPNs — then approach the same systems from an attacker's perspective.

I'm pursuing a career in offensive security and penetration testing, using platforms like TryHackMe and HackTheBox to sharpen practical skills outside coursework. All hands-on offensive practice happens in authorized lab environments only — never on systems I don't own or have explicit permission to test.

Currently focused on

  • Ethical Hacking Methodology
  • Vulnerability Assessment
  • Secure Network Architecture
  • TryHackMe / HackTheBox Labs
  • Cloud Infrastructure (AWS)
  • CTF Challenges
